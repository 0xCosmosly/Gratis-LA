# Strict No-Tip Discovery Methodology

## Goal

Find restaurants that meet the strict `No tip` rule:

- Official source says tipping is not expected, not accepted, or not allowed.
- No mandatory service fee is added to every bill.

Do not count:

- `Included` restaurants that add a mandatory fee to every bill.
- Large-party gratuity rules.
- Third-party reservation pages, Yelp, Reddit, or diner reports as proof.

## What Has Worked

### 1. Exact-phrase discovery on official domains

These phrases have produced the cleanest hits:

- `gratuity-free`
- `no tips are expected or accepted`
- `tips are not expected or accepted`
- `do not accept tips`

Generic searches like `no tipping restaurant los angeles` are too noisy.

### 2. Official newsroom, hospitality, and blog pages

Marketing and newsroom pages often publish the policy more clearly than the main menu page.

Current LA examples found this way:

- `Plateia at UCLA Luskin`
- `Epicuria at Ackerman`

### 3. Property-level hospitality PDFs

Official hospitality PDFs can contain strict no-tip language even when the named restaurant page does not.

Validated off-map example:

- `North Shore Tavern at UCLA Lake Arrowhead Lodge`
- Official dining PDF states: `The Lodge is a gratuity-free property. No tips are expected or accepted.`
- Not merged into the site because it is outside the current LA scope.

### 4. Operator-level discovery before location-level discovery

The highest-yield operators so far are:

- university hospitality groups
- conference centers
- hotel restaurants
- institutional dining programs

Search the operator first. If the operator publishes a strict no-tip rule, then expand to named dining locations.

## What Has Not Worked

### 1. Independent restaurant homepage scraping

Most independent restaurants either:

- publish nothing about tipping
- publish only large-party gratuity rules
- bury the language in non-indexed images or PDFs

### 2. Community and review sites as evidence

These are useful for leads only:

- Reddit
- Yelp
- Google reviews
- Eater / LAist / listicles

They are not reliable proof under the current rules.

### 3. Search-result snippets without current-page confirmation

Many snippets are stale. They often mention no-tip or gratuity-free language that is no longer present on the live official page.

## Current Best Workflow

1. Generate leads from:
   - exact-phrase web searches
   - university hospitality sites
   - institutional dining sites
   - official hospitality PDFs
2. Verify only on official sources.
3. Check for disqualifiers:
   - mandatory fee on every bill -> `Included`, not `No tip`
   - gratuity only for parties of `X+` -> reject
4. Merge only if the strict no-tip rule is explicit.

## Current LA Strict No-Tip Wins

- `Plateia at UCLA Luskin`
- `Epicuria at Ackerman`

## Current Constraint

The strict `No tip` pool is materially smaller than the `Included` pool under current LA scope and current proof rules.

If we want the `No tip` count to overtake `Included`, the next most defensible expansions are:

- more official university hospitality operators in Greater LA
- more public institutional dining programs
- more official hospitality PDFs tied to named public restaurants
- broader geography beyond the immediate LA map
